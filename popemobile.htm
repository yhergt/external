Secure. Contain. Protect.

Item #:███33

Object Class: Euclid

Special Containment Procedures:

SCP-██33 is to be kept within a standard vehicle storage garage. SCP-███33 may not be removed from containment or be fuelled except for testing. For safety reasons, only personnel of Catholic faith are permitted within 50m of SCP-███33 once fuelled. The land around SCP-███33-2 has been purchased by the Foundation and is closed off to the public under Cover Story 74 ("Private Property").

Description

SCP-███33 is an M1 Abrams main battle tank. Visually, SCP-███33 displays no aberrations from a typical M1 Abrams main battle tank. Notably, all serial numbers on SCP-███33's components match those used in ████████████, an M1 Abrams destroyed in 1991 during the Gulf War. Components on the underside of SCP-██33 roughly form the shape of a cross, although this does not appear to have any effect on the vehicle's operation. The interior is roughly consistent with other M1 Abrams main battle tanks manufactured during the 1990s, albeit covered with examples of Catholic imagery and corporate advertising for Honda. The phrase "Jesus Takes the Wheel" occurs frequently within SCP-███33. SCP-███33's main anamalous property is its fuel source. SCP-███33 only runs on a fluid similar in composition to water from the Jordan River in composition and debris present, and can not be fuelled through any conventional methods. Fuelling SCP-███33 is only possible by praying in proximity to or within it, or by making donations to SCP-███33-2. Once fuelled, SCP-███33 can be operated similarly to a normal tank; however, it may sometimes perform actions outside of the operators' control. These usually consist acts of agression towards non-Catholics; for more information, consult the Experiment Log.

SCP-███33-2 is a church in █████████, Utah. SCP-██33-2 resembles churches typical of its region. However, a single wooden box labelled "Donations" is present within the entrance to SCP-██33-2. This box is fused to the floor of SCP-██33-2 and has, to date, resisted all attempts to open, relocate or destroy it. A small slit 5x1cm in size is present on the top face of the box; when American currency is inserted, SCP-██33 is fuelled. Insertion of other items appears to have no effect. While locals claim that SCP-██33-2 was constructed in 18██, analysis of SCP-██33-2's construction produces an age of 2,0██ years. Investigation is ongoing.

Addendum - SCP-██33-2 Experiment Log

Experiment Results
A D-Class identifying as atheist was ordered to stand in front of SCP-██33. SCP-██33 was then fuelled. Before any personnel could board SCP-██33, SCP-██33 aimed its turret at the D-Class and fired. The D-Class died from a wound consistent with a Catholic rosary impacting his chest at Mach 7.
SCP-██33's main gun was manually fired. A 105mm high explosive round was fired. No anamolous properties reported.
A D-Class identifying as Lutheran was ordered to stand in front of SCP-██33. SCP-██33 was then fuelled. SCP-██33 started its engine and aimed its turret at the D-Class after 10 seconds with no input from personnel. The D-Class reported a strong urge to flee, which subsided after he travelled 50m from SCP-██33.
A D-Class identifying as Catholic was ordered to stand in front of SCP-██33. SCP-██33 was then fuelled. No response from SCP-██33. SCP-██33 could be operated as normal.
A D-Class identifying as atheist was ordered to enter SCP-██33. All hatches closed and locked behind the D-Class. When SCP-██33 was fuelled, the interior of SCP-██33 was filled with a gas identical in composition to mustard gas. Once the D-Class died from suffocation, SCP-██33's hatches opened and all gas was safely vented.
A D-Class identifying as Catholic was ordered to enter SCP-██33. No response from SCP-██33. SCP-██33 could be operated as normal.
